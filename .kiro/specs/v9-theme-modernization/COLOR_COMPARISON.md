## Key Differences Summary

### 🎨 Color Temperature
- **Old**: Cold, blue-tinted grays
- **New**: Warm, slate-based grays with subtle warm undertones

### 💪 Saturation & Vibrancy
- **Old**: Muted, conservative colors
- **New**: Vibrant, energetic colors (while maintaining professionalism)

### 🌓 Dark Mode Contrast
- **Old**: Text colors too dim (#959BA2 for body text)
- **New**: Much better contrast (#CBD5E1 for body text)

### 🎯 Semantic Clarity
- **Old**: Success color same as secondary (confusing)
- **New**: Each status color is distinct and recognizable

### 🏗️ Foundation
- **Old**: Custom gray scale with blue tint
- **New**: Tailwind Slate scale (industry-standard, proven)

## What Users Will Notice

### Immediate Visual Changes

1. **Warmer Feel**: The interface will feel less cold and clinical
2. **Better Readability**: Especially in dark mode with improved text contrast
3. **More Modern**: Colors align with contemporary design trends
4. **Clearer Status**: Success/warning/danger are more distinct
5. **Softer Backgrounds**: Less harsh, easier on the eyes

### Subtle Improvements

1. **Reduced Eye Strain**: Warm undertones in grays
2. **Better Hierarchy**: Improved contrast between text levels
3. **Visual Harmony**: Consistent saturation across colors
4. **Professional Polish**: Refined, sophisticated palette
